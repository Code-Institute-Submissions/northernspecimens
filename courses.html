Our Courses

We offer a wide range of courses for all levels and interests.

Jars

The easiest and quickest to learn - wet preservation is often completed using sterile fluids such as isopropyl alcohol, fermaldehyde and formaline.

Mounting

Dry mounting is abit more advanced, it requires tanning the animals removed skin and placing it back over a "form". Mummification is also possible in smaller animals by placing them in borax and salts for a long time.

Bone-Works

Preserving a skeleton involves destruction of the soft tissue using beetles or soft acids. The skeleton can then be mounted into its original shape using galvanised wire.
