About Us

What is Taxidermy?

Taxidermy is an art of preserving animals who have recently died, usually the animals have died of natural causes or in Victorian times, they were hunted as trophies. Nowadays, Taxidermy is a popular hobby for many people; it can be extremely rewarding and profitable if you get good at it!

Taxidermy is made in many ways. For example, mammals, reptiles and insects all have different methods behind their preservation. Large animals need to be mounted through a skinning/tanning process. Whilst insects need to be stuffed with cotton wool, soaked in ethanol and left to dry.

Our Ethics

These days all the recent specimens we acquire are responsibly sourced from local areas and all animals have died from natural causes. Therefore, we do not believe in preserving new trophies for those who have acquired skins/body mounts through game-hunting. However, we will restore old throphy mounts dated before 2000 A.D.

Our Services

Established in 2018: Northern Specimens has gathered a reputation for excellence. We offer a range of bespoke dry-mounting services, wet specimen services and pet preservation.

All of our specimens are ethically sourced, promoting the art of taxidermy, while maintaining biodiversity.
We donate a percentage of our profits to conservation charities, to aid in the preservation of endangered wildlife.
